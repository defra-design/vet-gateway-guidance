Become an Official Veterinarian (OV) or set up a new OV practice

How to get authorised as an OV, maintain your records and set up a new practice, and what to do if you will be TB testing.

From: Animal Plant and Health Agency

Official Veterinarians (OVs) are private practice veterinarians who are qualified to deliver government-funded statutory testing and other veterinary services on behalf of the Animal and Plant Health Agency (APHA).

APHA will authorise any person as an OV who:

  • is a full member or fellow of the Royal College of Veterinary Surgeons, or approved by the RCVS to practice veterinary medicine for a specified period of time in the UK as an EU national
  • holds the appropriate Official Controls Qualification (Veterinary) (OCQ(V))
  • is regarded by APHA as suitable for carrying out tasks on behalf of Ministers, taking into account any previous performance as an OV or convictions for offences under animal health or welfare legislation

All OVs are authorised under the Policy for Authorisation of Official Veterinarians in GB. You must be familiar with this document before becoming an OV and while you are authorised.

How to become an OV

To become an OV you must register with Improve International and complete the relevant OCQ(V) training. Before you do any training you should check which qualification(s) are needed for the work you'll be doing.

Learn more about OCQ(V)s at Improve International.

APHA will grant your OV authorisation when you've completed the training and have met all qualification requirements. If you're a new OV, APHA will then send your official OV stamp and unique OV number to you by post. You'll need to use your stamp on official documents to certify them.

Authorisation can take up to 10 working days. You'll be notified by email when it's granted. You must wait until your authorisation is confirmed before starting any OV work.

OV training costs

You or your employer will be responsible for training costs.

Prospective and current OVs working in Scotland may be eligible to claim a Scottish Government funded code for OCQ(V) - SS and OCQ(V) - TT training.

What to do if you'll be TB testing

If you'll be carrying out TB testing you must be authorised under the OCQ(V) - TT qualification. You may not need to do this for non-statutory TB testing in zoological conditions.

Training requirement for vets carrying out zoological TB testing

After you have completed the theory training you must nominate a supervisor and enrol in Sam, APHA's online system for recording TB test results.

Enrol or sign in to Sam

After you enrol in Sam you'll be given conditional authorisation so you can complete a period of practical training and assessment. When this is completed, APHA will grant full authorisation.

Maintaining your qualifications and authorisation

Your authorisation will be suspended if you do not maintain your qualification(s). Most qualifications must be revalidated every 4 years. Improve International will send you reminders of your revalidation deadline(s) by email. If you do not revalidate by the deadline your authorisation will be suspended and you'll have 6 months to revalidate it before it expires.

If your authorisation expires you'll need to complete your OV training again.

Extensions and exceptions

You can apply for an extension or exception if you cannot complete:

  • the OCQ(V) revalidation within the specified deadline, or
  • the OCQ(V) - TT training requirements within the the period of conditional authorisation

Extensions and exceptions can only be given in certain situations. Details can be found in the application form.

Apply for an extension or exemption to the training and revalidation requirments.

If your OCQ(V) - TT authorisation has expired within the past 4 years and you want to be reauthorised, you can apply to waive the requirement to repeat the practical training and assessment for reauthorisation. Evidence of any TB audit carried out in the past 4 years must be provided with your application.

Maintaining your OV records

You are responsible for making sure all your details recorded on Improve International are correct.

If you are registered on Sam and need to change your name or transfer to another practice

To change your details on Sam you must first update your Improve International account information. You must then complete:

  • the OV57 form for a name change, or
  • the OV58 form for a practice transfer

Your information will be processed and updated on Sam within 10 working days.

If you need a replacement stamp

If your OV stamp has been lost, damaged or stolen you must complete the OV45 form and send it to APHA's OV team. The OV team will send you a replacement stamp within 10 working days of receiving the form.

You'll need a lost property number for a stamp that's been lost.

If your stamp has been stolen you must report the theft to the police. If you are given a crime reference number you must include it on the OV45 form.

How to stop being an OV

If your OV status is no longer required you can either:

  • deregister as an OV, or
  • allow your OV authorisation to expire by choosing not to revalidate your qualification(s)

How to destroy your official OV stamp

When your authorisation ends you must destroy your official OV stamp. You can do this yourself or post it to APHA's OV team to do it for you. If you want to destroy your stamp yourself, you must:

  1. Peel off the rubber containing the unique number.
  2. Cut the rubber off and dispose of the rubber and number separately.
  3. Take a picture of the destroyed stamp and email it to CSCOneHealthOVTeam@apha.gov.uk.

Setting up a new practice

New practices must download and complete the new OV practice forms, then return them to APHA by post.

After the forms are returned, your local APHA office may decide to inspect your practice. When they have confirmed that your practice can be set up, APHA's OV team will complete the process and notify you. The OV team will ensure that all OV records are created and updated, and that relevant documentation is sent out.

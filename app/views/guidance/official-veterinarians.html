Become an Official Veterinarian or set up a new practice

How to authorise and register as an Official Veterinarian (OV), and maintain your OV records.

From: Animal Plant and Health Agency

Published 9 November 2023

Official Veterinarians (OVs) are private practice veterinarians who are qualified to deliver government-funded statutory testing and other veterinarian services on behalf of the Animal and Plant Health Agency (APHA).

How to become an OV

To become an OV you must register with Improve International and complete the relevant Official Controls Qualification (Veterinary) training.

If you were authorised as an OV or Local Veterinary Inspector (LVI) before 2014, you may be able to apply for grandfather rights in the corresponding OCQ(V)s.

Learn more about the different OV qualifications at Improve International.

APHA will grant your OV authorisation when you have completed the training. Authorisation can take up to 10 working days. You will be notified by email when it has been granted. APHA will then send your stamp to you.

OV training costs

You or your employer will be responsible for training costs. Prospective and current OVs working in Scotland may be eligible to claim a Scottish Government funded code for OCQ(V) - SS and OCQ(V) - TT training.

Maintaining your qualification

Most OVs will need to maintain their qualification by revalidating it every 4 years.

If you do not maintain your qualification, your authorisation will be suspended. You will then have 6 months to revalidate the qualification. If you do not revalidate within 6 months, the authorisation will expire and you will need to complete the full training course to become an authorised OV again.

What to do if you no longer need to be an OV

If your OV status is no longer required, complete the OV55 form and send it to APHA's OV team. Your information will be processed within 10 working days.

Setting up a new practice

New practices should download and complete the new OV practice forms, then return them to APHA by post.

If your practice will be TB testing you must also complete the Sam declaration form OVb37

After the forms have been returned your local APHA office may wish to inspect your practice. When it is confirmed that your practice can be set up, APHA's OV team will complete the set up process and notify you. The OV team will ensure that all OV records are created and updated, and that relevant documentation is sent out.

What to do if your practice will be TB testing

Sam is APHA's system for recording the reigstration of livestock species, work management and bovine tuberculosis (bTB). All new OVs and Approved Tuberculin Testers (ATTs) must register on Sam to submit TB test results.

The practice that you test for must be registered on Sam in order for you to be able to submit your TB tests results.

If you are becoming an ATT, when you complete your ATT theory training you will be prompted to register on Sam. Your authorisation as an ATT will not be granted until this is complete.

To register for access to submit TB test results electronically please follow the steps below:

  1. Sign up your practice and practice staff by contacting the OV team - 03000 200 301 / CSCOneHealthOVTeam@apha.gov.uk
  2. Follow the instructions on the letter you will be sent (see the Registration and Enrolment User Guide for more information)
  3. Register and enrol as an organisation for the AHVLA-ORC service
  4. Register and enrol as an agent for the AHVLA-AGT service
  5. Once registered, the Primary Contact should select the Manage services on the Government Gateway link to manage other's access to Sam.

Guidance

Become an Approved Tuberculin Tester (ATT) or Certification Support Officer (CSO)

How to get and maintain your Animal Health Paraprofessional (AHP) qualification, and what to do if you want to end your authorisation.

Contents

  1. Eligibility
  2. Training and requirements
  3. Maintaining your qualification and authorisation
  4. Maintaining your AHP records
  5. How to stop being an AHP

ATTs and CSOs are non-veterinary professionals who are authorised by the Animal and Plant Health Agency (APHA) to carry out statutory work. They are also known as Animal Health Paraprofessionals (AHPs).

ATTs perform TB skin testing of cattle in England and Wales.

CSOs support Certifying Officers for export health certification for animal products (excluding germplasm and live animals).

Eligibility

ATT eligibility

ATTs must:

  • be at least 18 years old when you apply
  • have passed identification and basic Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS) checks
  • have at least 6 months livestock handling experience
  • have at least 3 GCSEs or equivalent qualifications in maths, English, and a science subject or food production, or 3 years experience in a government regulatory role
  • have your employer declare that you're eligible to do the training

CSO eligibility

CSOs must:

  • be over the age of 18
  • have the right to work in the UK
  • have a basic DBS check with no recorded convictions
  • be proficient in English and able to communicate for the purposes of the role of an AHP
  • have no known conflict of interest in the proposed work

You must also have at least 3 A levels, one of which must be in a science or food technology subject. Equivalent qualifications, such as Scottish Highers or non-UK qualifications, and all UK university degrees, are also eligible.

If you do not have any of these qualifications, you may be able to apply for CSO eligibility based on relevant experience (ET222).

Training and requirements

The training, qualification and appointment of AHPs is set out:

  • for ATTs, in the Policy for Authorisation of Approved Tuberculin Testers (TR541)
  • for CSOs, in the Policy for APHA Authorisation of Export Certification Support Officers (CSOs) in Great Britain (ET175)

Register with Improve International

To access the AHP training you must register with Improve International and then choose:

  • the OCQ(AHP) - ATT, if you want to become an ATT
  • the OCQ(AHP) - CSO, if you want to become a CSO

Complete the qualification and training

All AHPs must complete an online course and pass the end of course exam.

ATTs must also complete practical training and an assessment, under the supervision of an Approved Veterinary Supervisor (AVS). APHA will give you conditional authorisation to carry out the practical training after you pass the exam.

Get authorised by APHA

When you have completed your qualification and practical training, Improve International will ask APHA's OV team to grant your full AHP authorisation.

You must be regarded by the Agency as suitable for carrying out tasks on behalf of Ministers, taking into account any previous performance as an official

Authorisation can take up to 10 working days. You'll be notified by email if you're successful. If you're a CSO, APHA will send your CSO stamp to your main practice address.

Authorisation requirements for ATTs

Authorised ATTs must carry out any testing as part of a veterinary business, under the supervision of an AVS and one or 2 deputy AVS (AVSd). You will be audited by your AVS 6 months after passing the practical assessment.

You must also enrol in Sam to submit TB test results online.

Maintaining your qualification and authorisation

AHP qualifications must be revalidated:

  • for ATTs, 2 years after the initial authorisation and then every 4 years
  • for CSOs, every 4 years

Your authorisation will be suspended if you do not maintain your qualification(s). Improve International will send you reminders of your revalidation deadline by email. If you do not revalidate by the deadline your authorisation will be suspended and you’ll have 6 months to revalidate it before it expires.

If your authorisation expires you’ll need to complete your training again to reauthorise.

Extensions and exceptions

You can apply for an extension or exception if you cannot complete:

  • the OCQ(AHP) revalidation within the deadline
  • the OCQ(AHP) - ATT training requirements within the period of conditional authorisation

Extensions and exceptions can only be considered in certain situations. Details can be found in the application form.

Request extension or exception to OCQ training requirement (TR588).

Maintaining your AHP records

You are responsible for making sure all your details recorded on Improve International are correct.

If you need a replacement CSO stamp

If your CSO stamp has been lost, damaged or stolen you must complete the OV45 form and send it to APHA’s OV team. The OV team will send you a replacement stamp within 10 working days of receiving the form.

You’ll need a lost property number for a stamp that’s been lost.

If your stamp has been stolen you must report the theft to the police. If you are given a crime reference number you must include it on the OV45 form.

If you're registered on Sam and need to change your name or transfer to another practice

To change your details on Sam you must first update your Improve International account information, and then complete:

  • the OV57 form for a name change, or
  • the OV58 form for a practice transfer

Your Sam details will be updated within 10 working days.

How to stop being an AHP

You can either:

  • deregister as an AHP (form OV56), or
  • allow your authorisation to expire by choosing not to revalidate your qualification(s)

How to destroy your official CSO stamp

When your authorisation ends you must destroy your official CSO stamp. You can do this yourself or post it to APHA’s OV team to do it for you. If you want to destroy your stamp yourself, you must:

  1. Peel off the rubber containing the unique number.
  2. Cut the rubber off and dispose of the rubber and number separately.
  3. Take a picture of the destroyed stamp and email it to CSCOneHealthOVTeam@apha.gov.uk.

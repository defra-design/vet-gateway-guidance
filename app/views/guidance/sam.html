This service allows you to:

  • view TB testing instructions online
  • create private tests for your clients
  • continue to use TB Master functionality
  • work offline if the internet is unavailable

  • 16 June (9am) to 17 June (8am)
  • 8 September (9am) to 9 September (8am)
  • 15 September (9am) to 16 September (8am)
  • 8 December (9am) to 13 December (8am)

To record data while Sam is unavailable you can use:

  • the electronic (excel) TB52 form
  • a paper copy of the TB52 form
  • an excel XML file for use in third party software, such as TB Master

All data will need to be uploaded or manually entered into Sam when it is available again. Guidance on how to access and use the TB52 form can be found in sections 10 and 12 of the Sam Official Veterinarian Practice Guide.

Support with using Sam

You can get support with accessing or using Sam by contacting the Sam support desk:

  • by phone on 03300 416999
  • by email at APHAServiceDesk@apha.gov.uk

If you have entered data into Sam incorrectly

To fix incorrect data in Sam, download and complete form GENO2 then email it to:

  • csc.TBsamdatafix@apha.gov.uk for County Parish Holdings (CPHs) in England
  • Apha.cymruwales@apha.gov.uk for CPHs in Wales
  • Apha.scotland@apha.gov.uk for CPHs in Scotland

How to enrol in Sam

A principal or partner from your veterinary practice must complete the OV59 Application for Sam access form for the practice and each individual who wants to enrol in Sam.

Post the OV59 form to:

OV Team

Animal and Plant Health Agency

Customer Service Centre

One Health County Hall

Spetchley Road

Worcester

WR5 2NP

Your Sam enrolment information (known facts letter AH046) will be sent to your practice address within 10 working days.

Enrol in Sam as a practice

Before you can use Sam your practice must enrol as an organisation for the AHVLA-ORC service.

Practices can then link with enrolled individuals through the ‘Manage my agents’ screen.

Enrol in Sam as an individual

OVs, ATTs and practice administrators must:

  1. Enrol in the AHVLA-AGT (agent) Sam service.
  2. Enter the first part of your Government Gateway agent ID. The second part of your agent ID will be generated for you.
  3. Link your agent ID to an OV practice account.

To view your agent ID and link to a practice you must choose to ‘log on and manage my account’. Contact the APHA service desk if you cannot find your agent ID or have forgotten your sign in details.

Entering information from your known facts letter

When you enrol in Sam you will be asked to enter the enrolment information from your known facts letter. The role and application ID must be entered in capitals.

ATTs must remove the second ‘E’ from the word ‘Tester’ when entering the enrolment information. It should read, “APPROVED TUBERCULIN TESTR”.
